Section 1

MPI Automation: Addressing Industry Challenges

Magnetic Particle Inspection remains the gold standard for detecting surface and near-surface defects in ferromagnetic components across aerospace, automotive, power generation, and heavy manufacturing. While MPI's physics-based approach is proven and reliable, the inspection process faces mounting pressures in modern manufacturing environments.

1.1 Manual Inspection Limitations

Operator Dependency and Consistency

  • Subjective interpretation of indications varies between inspectors
  • Performance degradation due to fatigue over extended shifts
  • Extensive training and certification requirements (ASNT SNT-TC-1A, ISO 9712)
  • Inter-operator variability affects reproducibility

Throughput Constraints

  • Inspection cycle time: 2–5 minutes per part (medium complexity)
  • Manual process creates production bottlenecks
  • Incompatible with high-speed continuous flow manufacturing
  • Limited scalability for volume production

Quality and Traceability Gaps

  • False negative rate: 5–15% (missed defects due to operator factors)
  • False positive rate: 10–20% (unnecessary rejections, increased scrap costs)
  • Manual documentation prone to errors
  • Difficult to aggregate quality data for statistical process control

Workforce Challenges

  • Skilled MPI inspectors increasingly difficult to recruit and retain
  • High labor costs in developed manufacturing markets
  • 24/7 shift coverage requires multiple certified personnel
  • Ergonomic concerns: UV exposure, repetitive handling, darkened environments

Machine Vision Solution for MPI

2.1 Machine Vision System Architecture

Modern machine vision systems for MPI integrate multiple technologies to replicate and enhance human inspection capabilities:

High-Resolution Industrial Cameras

  • Industrial-grade sensors optimized for fluorescent particle detection
  • High-resolution imaging (2+ megapixels) to capture fine crack details
  • High frame rates (20–60 fps) for moving inspection lines
  • Global shutter technology to eliminate motion blur
  • GigE or USB3 interface for high-speed data transfer
  • Optical filters (e.g., yellow-green) to enhance defect contrast

Specialized Illumination

  • UV-A LED arrays (365nm peak wavelength) for fluorescent particle excitation
  • Uniform intensity distribution across field of view (>1000 µW/cm² minimum)
  • Synchronized strobing for motion freeze in high-speed applications
  • Visible light supplementation for part localization and orientation

Machine Vision Computer / Controller

  • GPU-accelerated image processing for real-time analysis
  • Deep learning inference engines for defect classification
  • Edge computing capability for factory floor deployment
  • Industrial-grade hardware for harsh manufacturing environments

Factory Integration Interfaces

  • PLC communication (Modbus TCP/IP, OPC-UA, EtherNet/IP)
  • Proximity sensors and triggers for part presence detection
  • Rejection mechanisms (pneumatic gates, diverters, marking systems)
  • HMI displays for operator oversight and manual verification stations

Anomaly Detection: Optimized for Manufacturing Excellence

4.1 The Manufacturing Reality: Learning from Good Parts

In high-quality manufacturing, defects are rare (0.1–5% of production). Traditional AI approaches require thousands of labeled defect examples — practically impossible when you're producing good parts 95–99% of the time.

PixIQ's anomaly detection takes a fundamentally different approach: Instead of teaching the system what defects look like, we teach it what normal / good parts look like. Anything that deviates from normal is automatically flagged for review.

Key Advantage: You can deploy inspection systems with only 200–500 images of good parts — no defect examples needed upfront.

Section 6

Use Case: Connecting Rod Inspection with PixIQ

6.1 Application Overview

Connecting rods are critical engine components subject to extreme cyclic loads and stresses. Manufacturing defects — particularly surface cracks, laps, and seams — can lead to catastrophic engine failure. MPI is mandated for 100% inspection in automotive and aerospace applications.

ParameterDetail
MaterialForged steel — SAE 4340 or similar high-strength alloy
Critical AreasFillet radii at cap interface, beam section, pin end bores
Defect TypesForging laps, quench cracks, grinding cracks, material inclusions
Production Volume500–2,000 parts per shift
Quality Requirement0 DPMO for critical cracks

6.2 Traditional Inspection Challenges

  • Complex geometry requires inspection from 4–6 orientations
  • Inspection time: 3–5 minutes per part
  • Throughput bottleneck: ~150 parts/shift/inspector
  • False negative risk: 5–10% miss rate due to operator fatigue
  • 4 certified inspectors required for 24/7 coverage
  • Fine cracks (<0.5mm width) difficult to detect in complex geometry

6.3 PixIQ Automated Solution

Hardware Setup

  • Cameras: 1–4× Basler acA1920-40gc (2MP GigE) — multi-angle (cap end, pin end, side views)
  • Lens: 16mm fixed focal length per camera
  • Filter: Hoya X0 Yellow-Green for enhanced defect contrast
  • Triggering: Rotary indexing table with position encoder
  • Magnetization: Automated electromagnetic coil with AC magnetization
  • Part Handling: Robotic gripper with 6-axis articulation
  • Controller: Asus PE1100N — Orion 8GB

Software Configuration

  • AI Model: Anomaly detection (autoencoder) + U-Net segmentation
  • Training Data: 500 known-good connecting rods (2,000 multi-angle images)
  • Defect Classes: Crack, lap, seam, porosity, inclusion, non-defect
  • Decision Threshold: Tuned for <0.5% false positive, <1% false negative

Factory Integration

  • PLC: Allen-Bradley CompactLogix via EtherNet/IP
  • Rejection: Pneumatic diverter gate to reject chute
  • MES: REST API for production data upload
  • HMI: Asus AIO A3202 (Ubuntu 22.04) with touchscreen

6.4 Inspection Workflow

  1. Part Loading (Automated) — Robot picks from conveyor, orients in fixture, confirms orientation via visible-light pattern matching
  2. Magnetization & Particle Application (5 sec) — AC electromagnetic coil applies circular field; wet fluorescent suspension sprayed uniformly; excess drained
  3. Multi-Angle Inspection (2 sec) — Cameras simultaneously triggered; high-resolution images captured from all orientations (50ms total)
  4. AI Analysis (80ms) — Image transfer (20ms) → preprocessing (10ms) → anomaly detection in parallel (35ms) → U-Net segmentation (15ms) → PASS/FAIL fusion
  5. Result & Action (20ms) — PLC signal via EtherNet/IP; PASS to output conveyor, FAIL to reject chute, VERIFY to manual station
  6. Data Logging — All images stored with serial number & timestamp; defect coordinates & confidence recorded; HMI updated in real-time
  7. Manual Verification (if required, <2% of parts) — Operator views all images with AI-highlighted regions; confirms or overrides; feedback refines threshold
